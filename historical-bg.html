History

The Road Transport Employers Association of Nigeria (RTEAN), was first introduced in Bayelsa State by Chief Dr. Amb. Ifie Oyinkuro Ereboh and his team in the year 2001. On commencement of operations in 2001, Late Chief Ebikabowei Christopher Adokeme, who also served as the Bayelsa State Football Chairman became Pioneer Chairman of the union. During his tenure, he navigated challenges from various sectors, with a notable focus on establishing the union's distinct identity and uniqueness compared to sister unions.
Late Chief Ebikabowei Christopher Adokeme laid the foundational pillars that enabled RTEAN Bayelsa State chapter to emerge from the chaos and violence that once plagued the transport sector. He strategically developed new routes connecting the capital to interior towns and villages such as Imiringi, Ogbia, Otuosegha, Swali, and Amassoma. Among his significant achievements was the establishment of RTEAN's first general park at Tombia Junction, strategically positioned to facilitate inter and intra-state movements from the state's capital.
Chief Ebikabowei Christopher Adokeme passed away in 2021 after a brief illness, leaving behind a legacy of progress and development within RTEAN Bayelsa State chapter. Following Chief Adokeme's passing, Chief Dr. Amb. Ifie Oyinkuro Ereboh assumed the role of state chairman. With thanks to his experience as the state taskforce Chairman from 2003, Chief Ereboh was able to enforce law and order within various units, increased union registrations, and facilitated peaceful dialogues in resolving disputes between other unions and the state government's transport sector. His leadership during the task force era earned him respect and admiration from his executives and workers.
Upon his inauguration at RTEAN HOUSE, Abuja, Nigeria, by RTEAN National President Alhaji Musa Maitakobi 1, Chief Dr. Amb. Ifie Oyinkuro Ereboh swiftly resolved internal affairs, fostered unity among units in the state, expanded existing routes, established new parks, and appointed new executives to manage them effectively. His proactive approach and swift actions contributed significantly to RTEAN's reputation as a prominent employer of labor. Some of the newly established routes within the state include: Yenagoa to Ekeremor Yenagoa to Bomadi Yenagoa to Angiama Yenagoa to Sagbama Yenagoa to Toru Orua During Chief Dr. Amb. Ifie Oyinkuro Ereboh's chairmanship, he has successfully addressed major challenges, including resolving court cases between Bayelsa State Tricycle Riders Association (BITRA) and the three gazetted unions in Bayelsa State. He has also tackled disunity among the gazetted unions by establishing clear and fair rules beneficial to all parties involved. Within two years of his tenure, Chief Dr. Amb. Ifie Oyinkuro Ereboh has ascended to becoming the sixth Vice President of RTEAN and the South-South Zonal Chairman. He is currently engaged in restructuring, revitalizing, and promoting RTEAN's activities in the South-South region of the country.
